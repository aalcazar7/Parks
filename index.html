What is a park?:

Parks are places that can be used for many things such as human enjoyment and recreation, and some parks are for preservation and conservation. Most parks are either natural, semi-natural or small grass spaces for humans.

Types of parks and their differences:

Local Parks:

These types of parks are what you see in neighborhoods and around town, and are owned by the township. They are great places that you can visit daily with friends and family anytime. In the local parks page I will be talking about these types of parks. Check out my Local parks page to read a few reviews and get more information on some parks local to the Johnson county area.

State Parks:

State parks are owned by the state, and are great places to go for a day trip of fun outdoor time. These types of park offer more amenities such as cabins, boat rentals, atms, and many more depending on your location. Check out my State parks page to see some reviews and view links to more information for a few Indiana state parks!

National Parks:

National parks are typically larger than other types of parks, and their are fedraly owned by the tax payers of the United States. The money used by the tax payers is for the purpose of maintaining these area. National parks are procted lands with the purpose of preservation and can not be used for anything else.
